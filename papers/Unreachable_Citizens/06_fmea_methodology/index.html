    FMEA & Methodology¶

    ⚙️ Why Use FMEA?¶

    Budgets in fragile zones fail in predictable ways.
    To diagnose these failures, this study applies Failure Modes and Effects Analysis (FMEA) — a tool usually used in engineering and safety systems.

    Purpose of FMEA

    FMEA helps us see where, how, and why budgets break down, stage by stage.

    🛠️ Methodology Steps¶

    1. Identify failure modes

    2. Where do budgets fail? (e.g., missing records, stalled delivery, ghost allocations)

    3. Assess effects

    4. What happens when the failure occurs? (citizens excluded, funds wasted, services delayed)

    5. Rank severity and risk

    6. Which failures hurt most? Which are most likely to repeat?

    7. Propose mitigation

    8. Hybrid systems, analogue backups, visibility checks, community intermediaries

    Diagnostic Value

    By ranking failures, policymakers can prioritize fixes instead of chasing efficiency alone.

    🔍 Example Failure Modes¶

    • Birth registration missing → child invisible in education and health budgets
    • Migrant records unstable → workers excluded from welfare schemes
    • Unregistered deaths → families lose pension or insurance benefits

    Systemic Pattern

    These failures are not isolated.
    They repeat across life stages and institutions.

    📊 Methodological Lens¶

    This study combines FMEA with field typology and lifecycle matrix:

    • Typology shows where invisibility happens
    • Lifecycle matrix shows when invisibility happens
    • FMEA shows how invisibility breaks budgets

    Together, they create a multi‑lens diagnosis of civic exclusion.

    ✅ Key Takeaway¶

    FMEA is not just an engineering tool.
    It is a budgeting lens that helps governments see failures before they repeat.
