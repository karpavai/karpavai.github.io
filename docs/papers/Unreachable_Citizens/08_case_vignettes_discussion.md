# Case Vignettes & Discussion

---

## 📝 Why Case Vignettes?
Numbers and typologies tell us *where* exclusion happens.  
But stories show us *how* exclusion feels in real life.  
This section uses short vignettes to illustrate invisible citizens and broken budgets.

!!! note "Purpose"
    Case vignettes make the problem **human and relatable**, beyond statistics.

---

## 🏞️ Vignette 1: Tribal Birth
A child is born in a forest buffer zone.  
No hospital nearby, no digital record created.  
Years later, the child is invisible in school enrollment and health insurance systems.

!!! warning "Impact"
    One missing birth record → exclusion across education, health, and welfare.

---

## 🚧 Vignette 2: Migrant Worker
A family moves seasonally for construction work.  
Their ration cards don’t travel with them.  
Budgets assume they are “served” in their home district, but in reality they go hungry.

!!! danger "Impact"
    Mobility without visibility → wasted funds and unmet needs.

---

## 🌊 Vignette 3: Coastal Elder
An elder lives in a coastal village hit by repeated floods.  
Pension records are lost in damaged offices.  
The elder becomes invisible to the welfare system, despite decades of eligibility.

!!! info "Impact"
    Fragile infrastructure → citizens erased from long‑term entitlements.

---

## 🔎 Discussion
These vignettes show that invisibility is not abstract.  
It is lived daily by citizens in fragile zones.  
Budgets fail not because of corruption alone, but because **systems cannot see the people they serve**.

- Typology explains *where* invisibility happens.  
- Lifecycle matrix explains *when* invisibility happens.  
- Vignettes explain *how* invisibility feels.  

!!! tip "Key Takeaway"
    Policy must combine data with lived stories.  
    Only then can budgets be designed for **human visibility**.
