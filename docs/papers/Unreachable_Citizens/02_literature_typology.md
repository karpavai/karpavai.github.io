# Literature Gap & Zone Typology

---

## 📚 Literature Gap
Most studies on technology in governance celebrate efficiency, transparency, and speed.  
But very few ask a basic question: *what happens when citizens are invisible to the system itself?*  

!!! warning "Blind Spot in Research"
    Academic work often assumes that infrastructure is stable and citizens are digitally reachable.  
    In reality, millions live in fragile zones where these assumptions collapse.

---

## 🗺️ Zone Typology Overview
This study introduces a **typology of zones** to diagnose reachability:

- 🏞️ **Tribal / Forest regions** → disconnected from digital grids  
- 🏚️ **Urban slums** → unstable addresses, poor traceability  
- 🚧 **Migratory settlements** → citizens move faster than records  
- 🌊 **Coastal areas** → ecological stress disrupts continuity  
- 🛂 **Border zones** → fragile institutions, high exclusion risk  

!!! info "Diagnostic Purpose"
    The typology is not about geography alone.  
    It’s about **visibility** — whether citizens can be seen, traced, and included in budgets.

---

## 🔎 Why Typology Matters
- It shifts focus from *technology readiness* to *citizen visibility*.  
- It helps policymakers see where digital tools fail.  
- It creates a framework for hybrid systems that mix analogue and digital methods.

!!! tip "Key Takeaway"
    Digital tools fail when they ignore civic visibility and analogue institutions.
