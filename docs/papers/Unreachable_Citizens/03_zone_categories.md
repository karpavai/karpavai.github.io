# Zone Categories & Attributes

---

## 🏞️ Why Categorize Zones?
Not all “unreachable” areas are the same.  
Some are invisible because of geography, others because of mobility, and still others because of fragile institutions.  
By breaking them into categories, we can see **patterns of exclusion** more clearly.

!!! note "Purpose of Categorization"
    Categories help policymakers and nonprofits design **different strategies** for different invisibilities.

---

## 🗂️ Zone Categories
- **Tribal / Forest Zones**  
  Citizens live in deep ecological buffers. Connectivity is rare, and analogue records are the only trace.  

- **Urban Slums**  
  Addresses are unstable, homes shift, and official records often fail to keep up.  

- **Migratory Settlements**  
  People move faster than institutions can record them — seasonal workers, displaced families, mobile camps.  

- **Coastal Zones**  
  Ecological stress (floods, erosion, storms) disrupts continuity of records and services.  

- **Border Zones**  
  Fragile institutions, overlapping jurisdictions, and high risk of exclusion.

---

## 🔎 Attributes of Unreachable Zones
Across these categories, common attributes emerge:

- **Low visibility** → citizens are hard to trace in official systems.  
- **Fragile infrastructure** → electricity, internet, and transport are unreliable.  
- **High risk** → budgets fail to reach, leaving gaps in health, education, and welfare.  
- **Dependence on analogue tools** → paper records, local intermediaries, and oral testimony remain critical.  

!!! warning "Key Challenge"
    Technology assumes stability. These zones remind us that **instability is the norm** for millions.

---

## ✅ Key Takeaway
Categorization is not about labels.  
It is about **seeing the invisible** — so budgets can be designed to reach every citizen, no matter where they live.
