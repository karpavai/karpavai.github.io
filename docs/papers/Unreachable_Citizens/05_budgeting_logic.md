# Budgeting Logic

---

## 💰 Why Budgets Fail in Unreachable Zones
Budgets are designed with the assumption that citizens are visible in official systems.  
But when people live in fragile zones, the logic of budgeting itself breaks down.

!!! warning "Invisible Citizens"
    If citizens cannot be seen, they cannot be counted.  
    And if they cannot be counted, they cannot be budgeted for.

---

## 🔎 The Standard Logic
Most government budgets follow a **linear logic**:

1. Count citizens →  
2. Allocate funds →  
3. Deliver services →  
4. Monitor outcomes  

This works only when citizens are traceable in digital or official records.

---

## ⚠️ The Broken Chain
In unreachable zones, the chain collapses:

- **Counting fails** → births, deaths, migrations not recorded  
- **Allocation misfires** → funds assigned to “ghost” populations or missing groups  
- **Delivery stalls** → services don’t reach fragile infrastructures  
- **Monitoring is blind** → no feedback loops from invisible citizens  

!!! danger "Systemic Risk"
    Budgets built on incomplete visibility create **systemic exclusion**.

---

## 🔧 Hybrid Logic
This study argues for a **hybrid budgeting logic**:

- Mix **analogue tools** (paper records, local intermediaries, oral testimony)  
- With **digital systems** (apps, dashboards, portals)  
- Start with **visibility first**, not efficiency first  

!!! tip "Key Takeaway"
    Budgets must be designed for **inclusion before optimization**.
