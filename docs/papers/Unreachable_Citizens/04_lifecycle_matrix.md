# Lifecycle Matrix

---

## 🔄 Why a Lifecycle Matrix?
Budgets don’t fail only at one stage of life.  
They fail across the **entire journey of a citizen** — from birth to death.  
The lifecycle matrix helps us see where invisibility strikes at each stage.

!!! note "Purpose"
    The matrix is a diagnostic tool.  
    It maps **life stages** against **budget touchpoints** to show where exclusion happens.

---

## 👶 Life Stages & Budget Touchpoints
- **Birth** → registration gaps, missing birth certificates  
- **Education** → school enrollment failures, dropout invisibility  
- **Work** → informal labor, unrecorded wages, migrant instability  
- **Health** → fragile access to clinics, missing insurance records  
- **Elderhood** → pensions delayed, citizens lost in paperwork  
- **Death** → unregistered deaths, families excluded from benefits  

!!! warning "Critical Insight"
    Invisibility is not random.  
    It follows citizens through every stage of life.

---

## 📊 Matrix View
Think of the lifecycle matrix as a **grid**:

- Rows = **Life stages** (birth, education, work, health, elderhood, death)  
- Columns = **Budget systems** (registration, welfare, insurance, pensions, subsidies)  
- Cells = **Points of exclusion** where citizens drop out of visibility  

!!! info "Diagnostic Value"
    The matrix shows that exclusion is **systemic**, not accidental.  
    It repeats across different institutions and stages.

---

## ✅ Key Takeaway
Budgets must be designed with a **lifecycle lens**.  
Only then can we ensure that citizens remain visible from birth to death.
