# Institutional Feedback

---

## 🏛️ Why Institutional Feedback Matters
Budgets don’t exist in isolation.  
They are shaped by institutions — schools, hospitals, local offices, panchayats, and welfare boards.  
Feedback from these institutions shows where visibility breaks down in practice.

!!! note "Purpose"
    Institutional feedback connects **policy design** with **ground reality**.

---

## 🗂️ Sources of Feedback
- **Local schools** → report missing children who were never registered  
- **Health clinics** → highlight patients without insurance or ID cards  
- **Panchayats / local councils** → identify families excluded from welfare lists  
- **NGOs and community groups** → document invisible migrants and slum dwellers  
- **District offices** → struggle with incomplete records and fragile infrastructure  

!!! info "Diagnostic Value"
    Institutions act as **mirrors** — they reflect the gaps between budgets and citizens.

---

## 🔎 Patterns in Feedback
Across different institutions, common themes emerge:

- Citizens are **present physically** but **absent digitally**  
- Paper records remain **more reliable** than digital dashboards  
- Local staff often **know the invisible citizens**, but systems don’t capture them  
- Feedback loops are weak — reports rarely change budget allocations  

!!! warning "Systemic Risk"
    Without strong feedback loops, invisibility becomes **normalized** in governance.

---

## ✅ Key Takeaway
Institutional feedback is not just paperwork.  
It is a **diagnostic signal** that budgets must listen to, if they want to reach every citizen.
