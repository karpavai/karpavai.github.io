# SOP Maturity Index

The SOP Maturity Index describes the stages of adoption and optimisation of Standard Operating Procedures.  
It helps organisations assess their current state, identify gaps, and plan improvements.  
This framework is particularly useful for nonprofits and IT service teams seeking donor confidence, compliance, and operational clarity.

---

## Level 1: Ad‑hoc, Undocumented Process
- Processes are informal and undocumented.  
- Tasks vary depending on who performs them.  
- High risk of inconsistency and non‑compliance.

## Level 2: Basic SOPs, Informal Adoption
- SOPs exist but are not consistently followed.  
- Adoption is informal, with limited accountability.  
- Risk reduction is partial, and reproducibility is weak.

## Level 3: Documented SOPs, Periodic Review
- SOPs are documented and reviewed periodically.  
- Teams begin to follow standardised steps.  
- Compliance improves, but integration with external policies is limited.

## Level 4: Integrated SOPs with External Policies
- SOPs are aligned with external stakeholder requirements (regulators, donors, ISO frameworks).  
- Governance policies are embedded in daily operations.  
- Transparency and reproducibility are strengthened.

## Level 5: Optimised SOPs with Audit Cycles & Cost Modelling
- SOPs are fully optimised, embedded in audit cycles, and linked to budgeting models.  
- Continuous improvement is part of the culture.  
- Donor confidence and sponsor optics are maximised.

---

## Why the Maturity Index Matters
- Provides a roadmap for organisations to strengthen governance.  
- Helps nonprofits demonstrate accountability to donors.  
- Supports IT service teams in aligning with ISO and ITIL practices.  
- Encourages continuous improvement and sustainable programme delivery.
