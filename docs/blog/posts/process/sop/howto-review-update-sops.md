# How‑to: Reviewing and Updating SOPs

SOPs must be reviewed and updated regularly to remain relevant, compliant, and effective.  
This guide provides a practical checklist for nonprofit and IT service teams to keep SOPs aligned with governance and donor requirements.

---

## Step 1: Periodic Review
Schedule regular reviews (e.g., annually or bi‑annually).  
Ensure SOPs align with current governance policies and external stakeholder requirements.

## Step 2: Version Control
Maintain documented revision history with version numbers and dates.  
This supports audit readiness and transparency.

## Step 3: Update Triggers
Revise SOPs when:
- Regulations change  
- Donor requirements evolve  
- Project workflows are modified  

## Step 4: Engage Stakeholders
Include process owners and operations teams in the review cycle.  
Capture practical feedback to improve usability and adoption.

## Step 5: Pilot Testing
Validate updates through pilot runs before formal approval.  
This ensures changes are workable in real‑world conditions.

## Step 6: Approvals
Obtain approvals from governance committees or sponsor representatives.  
Formal sign‑off demonstrates accountability and compliance.

## Step 7: Communication
Communicate revisions clearly to staff and stakeholders.  
Provide training or briefing sessions to ensure consistent adoption.
