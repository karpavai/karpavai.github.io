Donor Reporting SOP

Purpose

To establish a structured process for reporting to donors, ensuring transparency, compliance with nonprofit regulations, and alignment with stewardship principles.

Scope

Applicable to all donor-funded projects under Society/Trust Act frameworks, including grants, CSR contributions, and individual donations.

Responsibilities

  • Board/Trustees: Approve donor reports before submission.
  • Finance Team: Prepare utilization certificates, budget vs actuals.
  • Program Team: Document outcomes, beneficiary feedback.
  • Compliance Officer: Ensure reports meet statutory and donor requirements.

Reporting Cycle

  • Quarterly Reports: Financial utilization + program updates.
  • Annual Reports: Consolidated financials, impact assessment, audited statements.
  • Ad-hoc Reports: On donor request or milestone completion.

Process Steps

  1. Collect Data: Program outcomes, financial utilization, beneficiary feedback.
  2. Draft Report: Align with donor format and statutory requirements.
  3. Review & Approval: Internal review by finance and governance committees.
  4. Submission: Send to donor within agreed timelines.
  5. Archival: Store reports with version control for audit readiness.

Compliance Alignment

  • Society/Trust Act: Maintain transparency in fund utilization.
  • ISO 9001: Documentation consistency and reproducibility.
  • Donor Agreements: Tailor reporting to contractual obligations.

Outputs

  • Donor report (quarterly/annual)
  • Utilization certificate
  • Impact summary
  • Audit-ready documentation
